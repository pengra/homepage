Cross-site scripting attacks usually occur when 1) data enters a Web app through an untrusted source (most often a Web request) or 2) dynamic content is sent to a Web user without being validated for malicious content. XSS attacks can be put into three categories: stored (also called persistent), reflected (also called non-persistent), or DOM-based.
